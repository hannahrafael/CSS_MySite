A Few of my Favorite Projects:

Iterative Design Project – ND Picture Frame

During the Fall of 2018, I worked on a team of three members to create a 3D printed picture frame for the University bookstore using SolidWorks and Makerbot. The first iteration of this frame, believe it or not, was rated one of the lowest in my Intro to Engineering Systems class. By considering the feedback my team receive from our peers, instructor, and bookstore employees, we redesigned the frame and completed a second iteration.

I am most proud that our frame ultimately won “Best Overall Design” in our class, and is currently displayed in Stinson-Remick Hall of Engineering at school.

Face Shield Design


In light of the COVID-19 pandemic (2020), one of my class projects was to design a face shield for medical personnel as additional PPE. I worked on a team with three other people remotely, and we collaborated over Zoom and group messaging in order to discuss our design and delegate the workload. Our main focus was not only the customer’s protection, but comfort as well.

Using SolidWorks, several iterations of the face shield were created, and the engineering drawings were also generated for submission. Additionally, my group wrote deliverables and created a Google Slides presentation that was presented over Zoom to our instructor, graduate teaching assistants, and peers. Afterwards, the designs were sent to Notre Dame’s 3D printing lab for further evaluation.

Processing Computer Game Project

During my summer in Italy, my final project for my Creative Programming class was to design and code a user-interactive computer game. I decided to base my project on one of my favorite PC Browser games growing up, “Log Runner.” By utilizing the features on Processing, I was able to create and adjust the aesthetic layout and character design of my game. Additionally, I was able to assign the keyboard functions to typical commands in computer games- such as pause, unpause, restart, and move-with-keyboard. While not required, I also incorporated dynamic difficulty adjustments (DDA) to automatically adapt the game’s difficulty based on the player’s skill.

To play try the game out yourself, feel free to click the download button above! Then, simply open the Processing file labeled "Final Project." Enjoy!
